A history of higher Education

Universities - Past, Present, Future

The first Universities

The first recorded universities were situated in europe primarily in central europe predominantly in Italy which at the time was at the heart of the Holy Roman Empire, and England where the culture around higher education grew out of their strong theological influences. The first of these early universities was the University of Bologna in northern Italy.

Colonialist Expansion

As countries like England and Spain expanded their sphere of influence and slaughtered and enslaved the indigenous peoples of the Americas, Africa, Asia and Oceania they brought science and education with them. First in the Americas and later in the rest of the world universities were founded.

The current State

Nowadays every major country in the world is home to at least one university with the exception of Antarctica. The United States have overtaken everyone else sporting an impressive 2000+ institutions of higher education with national recognition.

The United States

With a grand total of over 2000 accredited Universities and insitituations of higher education some states in the US got more Universities than many other countries. They are also home to 8 out of the 10 highest ranked Universities in the world. They are also world leaders in average tuition.

Education & Population

In this section you can explore how countries with booming populations like China and India deal with the increasing need for higher education as more and more people see their path out of poverty in a higher education. As more jobs with low entry requirements are made obsolete by machines a higher education secures peoples future.
